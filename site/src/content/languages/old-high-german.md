## Dictionaries

- [Old High German English Dictionary](https://en.glosbe.com/goh/en)
  - Notes: You can add phrases of your own, as well; it's a collaborative effort.

- [English-Old High German Dictionary](https://www.koeblergerhard.de/germanistischewoerterbuecher/althochdeutscheswoerterbuch/neuenglisch-ahd.pdf)
  - Format: PDF
  - Notes: A large PDF file with a ton of words.

- [Zedler Lexikon](https://www.zedler-lexikon.de/)
  - Notes: Something like the Oxford English Dictionary, for German, 300 years ago.

- [Grimm's Dictionary](https://woerterbuchnetz.de/?sigle=DWB)
  - Notes: From about 150 years ago, and pretty easy to use.

- [Etymological Dictionary of the German Language](https://archive.org/details/etymologicaldict00kluguoft)
  - Format: PDF
  - Notes: An old PDF, but has a lot of old information.

- [Early New High German Dictionary](https://web.archive.org/web/20231016102148/http://www.germanstudies.org.uk/enhg_dic/enhg_dica.htm)
  - Notes: An easy-to-navigate web interface.

- [Middle High German Conceptual Database](http://mhdbdb.sbg.ac.at:8000/index.en.html)
  - Notes: A pretty steep learning curve; probably best for only serious research.

## Texts

- [Old High German Texts](https://web.archive.org/web/20230924060857/http://texte.mediaevum.de/ahd.htm)
  - Notes: A site with many different texts, though it takes a bit of work to navigate.

- [Old German Texts](https://www.hs-augsburg.de/~harsch/germanica/Chronologie/d_chrono.html)
  - Notes: Separated by century.

- [Projekt Gutenberg](http://www.projekt.gutenberg.de/)
  - Notes: Contains many texts, a lot of which don't date back that far.

- [Austrian Literature Online](https://www.literature.at/collections.alo)
  - Notes: Some of these texts to date back a long way.

- [Anthology of Medieval German Literature](https://web.archive.org/web/20230924060857/http://www3.nd.edu/~gantho/anth1-163/frames.html)
  - Notes: Originally a book, it looks like, and easy to navigate.

- [St. Gall's Treasure Trove](https://www.e-codices.unifr.ch/en/list/subproject/stgall_oldhigh_german)
  - Notes: A collection of Old High German texts. 4 Medieval Manuscripts in German] These give a good idea of the handwriting, too.

- [Tatian in Old High German](http://lexicon.ff.cuni.cz/texts/ohg_sievers_tatian_about.html)
  - Notes: One of the earliest translations of Tatian's works.

- [Enlightenmen-era journals in German](https://www.ub.uni-bielefeld.de/diglib/aufklaerung/)
  - Notes: This is for serious scholarship, and you'll need to know German as well.
