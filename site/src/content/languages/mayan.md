## Dictionaries

- [Mayan Comparative Vocabulary](https://web.archive.org/web/20221113182341/http://maya.hum.sdu.dk/index.html)
  - Notes: Large comparative vocabulary database. Guest access is available. See the site’s help/info pages for notation and entry format details.

- [Dictionary of Maya Hieroglyphs](https://www.famsi.org/maintenance.html)
  - Notes: A very nice list, and easy to get around.

- [Spanish-Mayan Dictionary](https://www.mayas.uady.mx/diccionario/index.html)
  - Notes: Knowing Spanish is going to help for a lot of Mayan resources.

- [Swadesh List](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Swadesh_lists_for_Mayan_languages)
  - Notes: Lots of common words in Mayan languages.

- [Vocabulary Zinacantán Tzotzil](https://wold.clld.org/vocabulary/33)
  - Notes: With over 1000 entries, this can be a good choice.

- [Vocabulary Q'eqchi'](https://wold.clld.org/vocabulary/34)
  - Notes: Nearly 2000 entries in this one.

- [K'iche'-English Dictionary](https://www.famsi.org/maintenance.html)
  - Format: PDF
  - Notes: A very nice PDF file with lots of entries.

- [Mayan Mini-Dictionaries](https://www.native-languages.org/fammay.htm)
  - Notes: Choose a dialect from the page to access short word lists.

- [A short Itza-Maya vocabulary](https://www.famsi.org/maintenance.html)
  - Format: PDF
  - Notes: In PDF format.

- [Mam-Spanish dictionary](https://web.archive.org/web/20221113182341/http://www.cscd.osaka-u.ac.jp/user/rosaldo/Mam_Esp_DICC_COM.html)
  - Notes: All on the same page, with quite a few entries.

- [English-Yucatec Dictionary](https://www.mostlymaya.com/)
  - Notes: A decent-sized wordlist.

## Grammars

- [Mayan Grammar Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mayan_languages#Grammar)
  - Notes: Quick overview.

- [Tzeltal Grammar](https://web.archive.org/web/20221113182341/http://www.tzeltal.org/cgi-bin/stuart/tzeltal/static_page.cgi?page=download)
  - Format: PDF
  - Notes: A grammar of the spoken language; it's a rich-text file, so you'll need a word processor, or something like that. The Spanish version is available in PDF.

- [Tzotzil Grammar](https://web.archive.org/web/20221113182341/http://zapata.org/tzotzil/)
  - Notes: This can get confusing quickly, with all the dialects!

- [A Grammar of the Yucatecan Mayan Language](https://www.famsi.org/maintenance.html)
  - Notes: Nicely organized, so it's easy to follow.

- [A Maya Grammar](https://archive.org/details/mayagrammar09tozzrich)
  - Format: PDF
  - Notes: An old book, from 1921, in PDF format.

- [A Grammar of the Cakchiquel Language of Guatemala](https://archive.org/details/rosettaproject_cak_morsyn-1)
  - Format: PDF
  - Notes: Part of the proceedings of an organization, in PDF.

- [Basic Yucatec Maya](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tum7UWov0rI)
  - Format: Video
  - Notes: Youtube videos.

- [Mayan Language for Beginners](https://web.archive.org/web/20221113182341/https://www.yucatanliving.com/yucatan-survivor/mayan-language-for-beginners.htm)
  - Notes: Not sure how helpful this will be if you really want to study it in-depth.
